## Warsaw, 1830 — Overview

In the autumn of 1830 Warsaw is a city of roughly 140,000 inhabitants, the political and cultural capital of the Kingdom of Poland. The city is bounded on the east by the Vistula river, with the suburb of Praga on the eastern bank reached by the long Praga Bridge. The historic core lies on the western bluff: the Old Town, the Castle Square, and the wedge of streets running south.

The fashionable axis of the city runs north-to-south along the line of three connected streets — **Krakowskie Przedmieście**, **Nowy Świat**, and **Aleje Ujazdowskie** — known collectively as the Royal Route (Trakt Królewski). The grand palaces, the university, the cathedral, and the principal salons all lie along or just off this axis.

To the west of the Royal Route is the merchant district around **Senatorska** and **Marszałkowska**. To the north, beyond the Old Town, is **Bonifraterska Street**, the hospitals, and the Jewish district around Nalewki. To the far south, past the Belweder Palace, is the royal park of **Łazienki**.

The city is overlooked by Russian military presence: Cossack patrols, sentries at the bridges, the Russian-staffed Belweder, and the artillery park at the Saxon Square. The mood in autumn 1830 is one of accumulating tension, although the surface of social life — balls, theatre evenings, salon dinners — proceeds as if nothing were wrong.

## Streets and Districts

### Krakowskie Przedmieście

The grand boulevard running south from Castle Square. The Royal Castle stands at its northern end; the Saxon Garden gates, the Bristol's predecessor (the Tarnowski Palace in 1830), the University, the Visitationist Church, and the Holy Cross Church line its course. In the evening, the carriages of the wealthy pass along it almost continuously. Cossack patrols are stationed at intervals.

**Player visits:** the Holy Cross Church (Marianne's confession scene); the University courtyard (Tadeusz's encounter with Dr. Reszka in Act II); the carriage promenade on the night of the salon at Krasiński Palace.

### Nowy Świet (New World Street)

The continuation of Krakowskie Przedmieście southward. Less monumental, more commercial, with the workshops of fashionable artisans, several confectioners (notably Wedel's predecessor establishments), and the recently fashionable apartments of the new bourgeoisie.

### Aleje Ujazdowskie

The southern continuation, leading past the Ujazdów Hospital toward Belweder and Łazienki. Tree-lined, less built-up, fashionable for morning rides.

### Senatorska Street

A grand western counter-axis. The Krasiński Palace, the Treasury Palace, the Ministry of Internal Affairs, several great noble houses including the Sobański mansion. Closes the western side of Theater Square and the Saxon Square.

### Marszałkowska Street

A long north-south axis to the west of the Royal Route, connecting the Saxon Square down to the southern suburbs. More mixed in character, with shops, traders, and the kind of inns where army officers drink.

### Świętojańska Street

A short street in the Old Town leading from Castle Square to St. John's Archcathedral. Lipiński's apothecary stands at number 14 (fictional, in a real building footprint).

### Bonifraterska Street

North of the Old Town, in the New Town district. The Hospital of the Order of St. John of God (Bonifratrów Hospital) stands on this street. Bordering the Jewish district to the west.

### Solec

A working-class district along the Vistula bank, south of the Powiśle district, with breweries, tanneries, and warehouses. The conspirators' signal fire on November 29 will be set in a real brewery here. The player visits Solec in Tadeusz's storyline (a cholera-suspected case in a worker's tenement).

### Praga (across the Vistula)

The eastern suburb. Reached by the Praga Bridge. Less developed than the western city. Important for travel routes to the east. Russian military barracks and depots are concentrated here. In Antoni's storyline, an evening crossing is dramatically necessary.

## Major Buildings

### The Royal Castle (Zamek Królewski)

The historic seat of the Polish kings, now occupied during the Tsar's rare visits and used by the Polish administration. The Sejm sits in the Senate Chamber. The throne room is preserved. Tourists are not admitted; nobles with court connections may visit on certain days.

**Player visits:** Antoni attends a reception in the Marble Room (Act II). Marianne attends the Sejm opening from the public gallery in the prologue.

**Visual reference:** the building as restored c. 1820, before the post-Uprising depredations. The Sigismund Column stands in the Castle Square in front of the main gate.

### Belweder Palace

The residence of Grand Duke Constantine and Princess Joanna of Łowicz. A modest classical palace on Aleje Ujazdowskie, set in its own park. Heavily guarded by a mixed Russian-Polish detachment. The site of the November 29 attack.

**Player visits:** Antoni's official appearances (twice in Acts II and III). The night of November 29 — three radically different sequences depending on choice.

**Layout reference:** ground floor (state rooms), first floor (private apartments). The famous escape route — through a back door into the gardens, then to the Łazienki — is canonical and historically accurate.

### Saxon Palace (Pałac Saski) and Saxon Square (Plac Saski)

The Saxon Palace fronts the great open square that holds the Russian military review ground and the city's main artillery park. The square is one of the largest in Europe. Constantine reviews troops here in fair weather, and the Russian general staff have offices in the palace's eastern wing. The Polish General Staff is housed in the Brühl Palace adjoining.

**Player visits:** the parade scene in the prologue; the artillery park at night during the Uprising (depending on path).

**Cultural note:** this is a politically charged space. To walk across the square is to be reminded of who is in charge.

### Krasiński Palace (Pałac Krasińskich)

A grand baroque palace on Theater Square, owned by the Krasiński family. In 1830 it is open for occasional charity balls and salon evenings. The opening salon of the game takes place here, where Marianne and Antoni first meet.

**Player visits:** the prologue salon. A second visit in Act II for a charity concert.

### Krasiński Square, the Theatre on the Square (Teatr Narodowy)

The National Theatre stands on Krasiński Square (since 1833 it would move to the new Teatr Wielki, but in 1830 the older theatre is still in use). Nightly performances of opera, drama, ballet. Society goes to be seen as much as to watch.

**Player visits:** Marianne attends a performance of *Krakowiacy i Górale* in Act I; Antoni meets her at the interval.

### St. John's Archcathedral (Archikatedra Świętojańska)

The principal church of Warsaw, on Świętojańska Street near the Castle. Gothic in origin, much remodeled. Ceremonial functions of the city take place here.

**Player visits:** the requiem mass in Act III; Tadeusz meets Father Wiśniewski here in Act II.

### Holy Cross Church (Kościół Świętego Krzyża)

On Krakowskie Przedmieście. Fashionable for confession and society weddings. Marianne attends Sunday mass here when in town.

### University of Warsaw

Founded 1816. Located on the grounds of the former Kazimierzowski Palace on Krakowskie Przedmieście. Faculties of law, medicine, philosophy, natural sciences. Several thousand students, predominantly noble and townsman in background, with a strongly patriotic culture.

**Note for narrative:** the university will be closed by Tsarist order in 1831 in retaliation for the Uprising. In 1830 it is at its peak.

### Bonifratrów Hospital (Szpital Bonifratrów)

Hospital of the Order of St. John of God, on Bonifraterska Street. Founded 1650. Run by the Bonifratrów (Brothers of St. John of God) with assistance from lay nurses and tertiaries. Tadeusz's place of work.

**Player visits:** Tadeusz's main location across all acts. Marianne visits in Act III when she searches for her wounded brother.

### Ujazdów Hospital (Szpital Ujazdowski)

Military hospital south of the city, near Aleje Ujazdowskie. Mainly serves the Polish Army. Tadeusz consults here on Dr. Reszka's introduction.

### The Arsenal (Arsenał)

The artillery and small-arms depot at Długa Street, north of the Saxon Square. Polish troops took the Arsenal in the small hours of November 30 — a critical moment of the Uprising. Player visits during the climactic sequence.

### Sapieha Barracks (Koszary Sapieżyńskie)

On Zakroczymska Street. Quarters of the 4th Line Infantry Regiment, where Stefan Dembowski is posted. Tadeusz visits in Act II to deliver a letter; Marianne visits in Act III.

## Specific Game Locations

### Dembowski Manor (Dwór Dembowskich)

**Fictional location.** A small, white-walled manor house in the late Polish baroque style, two stories, columned portico, set in a half-overgrown park about thirty kilometers north-east of Warsaw, beyond the village of Dembowo. The manor commands a single mortgaged village and an aging orchard. The interior is comfortable but threadbare: faded carpets, family portraits with chipped frames, a library of pre-1815 French and German books, a sword over the fireplace that Józef Dembowski carried at Smoleńsk.

**Player visits:** the proposal scene in Act I; the family Christmas Eve sequence in the epilogue (if applicable).

**Atmosphere note:** the manor must read as *home* — warm, lived-in, a little shabby — even when it is the site of a painful scene. The contrast with Sobański's mansion is intentional.

### Sobański Mansion (Pałac Sobańskich w Warszawie)

**Semi-fictional.** Antoni's residence on Senatorska Street: a neo-classical mansion with a courtyard, a cold marble entrance hall, an enfilade of reception rooms, a library that is largely for show, and private apartments on the second floor. Decorated with a careful mixture of French and Russian taste, signaling the owner's cosmopolitan sympathies. The household includes a French cook, a Russian valet, a Polish housekeeper, and assorted maids and footmen.

**Player visits:** the dinner scene in Act II (Tadeusz attends); the late-night confrontation in Act III (Marianne arrives unannounced).

### The Brewery on Solec

**Real building, fictional name.** A working brewery owned by a Mazovian-German family, in an unremarkable yard off Solec Street. Conspirators have arranged for a fire to be set in the brewery's wood store on the evening of November 29 — the agreed signal that will indicate to all conspirators that the operation has begun. Whether this signal works as intended depends on the player's choices.

**Player visits:** Stefan Dembowski sequence in Act III. Marianne, depending on path, may end up here.

### The Conspirator Safehouse

**Fictional.** A second-floor apartment above a bookbinder's workshop on a side street off Krakowskie Przedmieście, rented under a false name. Used for meetings of an inner circle of conspirators. Plain, two rooms, a stove, a table, a small library that includes Mickiewicz's *Konrad Wallenrod* (a coded badge).

**Player visits:** Tadeusz's induction in Act II; possibly Marianne in Act III.

### Café Honoratka

**Real location (still standing today on a different site).** A café near Krakowskie Przedmieście where students, junior officers, and aspiring men of letters gather to read newspapers and argue. A real institution of pre-Uprising Warsaw. Tadeusz frequents it; Marianne is once smuggled in for a conversation.

## Surrounding Areas

### Łazienki Park

The royal park immediately south of Belweder. The Palace on the Water (Pałac na Wyspie) sits at its center, the work of King Stanisław August. In autumn 1830 the park is open to the public for walks. Constantine often rides here in the early morning.

**Player visits:** a romantic scene between Marianne and Antoni in Act II; possibly a chase sequence on the night of November 29.

### Wilanów

The summer palace of King Jan III Sobieski, twelve kilometers south of Warsaw, owned in 1830 by the Potocki family. A baroque palace and gardens of considerable beauty. The setting of a single set-piece: a hunt and ball in Act II that Antoni and Marianne both attend.

### The Vistula river and the Praga Bridge

The Praga Bridge in 1830 is a wooden pontoon bridge, vulnerable to ice and flood. The crossing is patrolled. The river itself carries barge traffic and (in summer) ferries.

### The road north to Dembowo

The post road from Warsaw runs north-east through several villages — Marki, Radzymin — before reaching the smaller back road to Dembowo. A six-hour journey by good carriage in dry weather; longer if the autumn rains come.

### The village of Dembowo

The single village belonging to the Dembowski estate. Forty households, a church, a cemetery, the priest Father Wiśniewski, an aging miller, and a steward who is fond of Marianne. The village is canonically Mazovian, with the regional dialect features (some -ej forms, occasional softened consonants) that flavor the speech of NPCs in the rural questline.
