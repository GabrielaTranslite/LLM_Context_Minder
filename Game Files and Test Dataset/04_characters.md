## Player Characters

### MARIANNE DEMBOWSKA

**Full name:** Marianna Helena Dembowska herbu Lubicz
**Common form used in source text:** Marianne (the family uses the French form in social settings; servants and rural neighbors use the Polish *Marianna* or *panna Marianna*)
**Age:** 19
**Class:** Szlachta, lower-middle, the upper edge of the impoverished category
**Faith:** Catholic, observant but not pious
**Birthplace:** Dembowo Manor, north of Warsaw, in the Mazovia plain

**Family situation.** Her father, Józef Dembowski, is a veteran of the Napoleonic campaigns who came home with a French sabre, a slight limp, and a reputation for stubborn patriotism that has not made him friends in Russian-aligned circles. The family estate has been mortgaged twice. Her mother, Zofia née Komorowska, manages the household with anxious care and a permanent ledger. Marianne has an older brother, **Stefan**, who is a lieutenant in the Polish 4th Line Infantry Regiment (and, unbeknown to the family, peripherally connected to the Cadet School conspiracy through old school friendships). She has a younger sister, **Helenka**, age twelve.

**The dilemma.** The Dembowski estate cannot survive another season without an injection of money. Marianne has a suitor: **Count Antoni Sobański**, twelve years her senior, wealthy, charming, and known to be cooperating with the Russian administration. A marriage would secure her family for a generation. But her brother is in danger; her father would be ashamed; and Marianne herself has been raised on her father's stories of the Legions, of Kościuszko, of the Princess Wanda who drowned rather than marry a foreign prince. The patriotic education that made her family proud now makes her offered marriage feel like treason. The personal twist: Sobański, despite himself, is genuinely falling for her, and she — despite herself — is not entirely indifferent to him.

**Voice and personality.** Marianne is intelligent, articulate, well-read in French and German, and capable of considerable irony. She is not naive about her position. She is not a fragile, delicate noblewoman of the romance-novel type — she has helped her mother run a struggling estate since she was fourteen, she rides hard, she keeps the household's accounts. Her dialogue should be educated, sometimes lyrical, capable of code-switching from formal Polish to French (in salon scenes) to a more direct, country-accented Polish (with servants and rural neighbors). She is *not* given to swearing; her exasperation comes through in dryness, not coarseness.

**Key beats players will encounter:**
- Opening: a salon at the Krasiński Palace where she first meets Sobański.
- The proposal scene at Dembowo Manor.
- The discovery of her brother Stefan's involvement.
- A choice point in late November: warn Sobański of the plot, or do not.
- The night of November 29 plays out very differently depending on prior choices.

**Localization notes.** Marianne's source-language dialogue uses some French phrases for character flavor (*comment dirais-je*, *mon cher*) — these should be retained in target text where naturally possible (a Polish noblewoman of 1830 would speak French) rather than translated. Her form-of-address with Sobański shifts from formal *Pan* to more intimate registers across the romance arc; see Style Guide §4.2 for the staircase of intimacy in Polish dialogue.

### TADEUSZ LIPIŃSKI

**Full name:** Tadeusz Wojciech Lipiński
**Age:** 26
**Class:** Mieszczaństwo (townsman class)
**Faith:** Catholic, increasingly skeptical
**Birthplace:** Warsaw, Old Town

**Family situation.** Tadeusz is the eldest son of **Wojciech Lipiński**, an apothecary with a successful shop on Świętojańska Street near the cathedral, and **Maria née Lewandowska**. His father has built the family's modest fortune over thirty years, and is fiercely proud of his son — the first Lipiński to attend university. Tadeusz studied medicine at the University of Warsaw (1822–1826), then spent two years in Berlin at the Charité, where Hufeland was the leading clinical figure of the day. He returned to Warsaw with continental credentials, modern training in clinical observation under Christoph Wilhelm Hufeland at the Charité, and an unease with the more traditional Polish medical establishment.

**Position.** He is on the staff of the Hospital of the Order of St. John of God (Bonifratrów Hospital) on Bonifraterska Street, working under the chief physician **Dr. Henryk Reszka** — a man old enough to be his grandfather, deeply patriotic, and (Tadeusz will discover) a long-standing member of the patriotic underground.

**The dilemma.** Tadeusz wants entry into the Warsaw salons. He resents being patronized by men whose only distinction is having grandfathers. He has spent his savings on tailored clothes, on horseback riding lessons, on a French dancing master. Marianne Dembowska, whom he met at a charity ball at the Krasiński Palace, has become both an aspiration and an obstacle: she sees through him with disconcerting kindness. As November approaches, his mentor Dr. Reszka draws him into a circle that turns out to be more than a literary discussion club. Tadeusz must decide whether to compromise his hard-won respectability for a cause his upbringing did not particularly emphasize.

**Voice and personality.** Tadeusz is observant, articulate, sometimes pedantic. He is a *modern* speaker in the early-19th-century sense — interested in chemistry, anatomy, public health, statistics. He uses Latinate vocabulary with patients (and is sometimes mocked for it by the older nurses). He is acutely sensitive to social slights. He drinks more than he should. He has a streak of self-deprecating humor.

**Key beats:**
- The cholera scare of October 1830 and the hospital's preparations (a real concern: the 1831 epidemic was already advancing west).
- A patient who turns out to be a wounded conspirator.
- A formal dinner at Sobański's mansion, where Tadeusz is the only townsman in the room.
- Choosing whether to treat a Russian officer wounded on the night of November 29.

**Localization notes.** Tadeusz's medical vocabulary uses Latin for technical terms (*pulsus*, *febris continua*) — preserve these in target text. His dialogue includes a few German tags acquired during his Berlin years; these should be left as German. He addresses Marianne with strict propriety even in his interior monologue; see Style Guide §4.2.

### COUNT ANTONI SOBAŃSKI

**Full name:** Antoni Marian Sobański herbu Junosza
**Age:** 31
**Class:** Magnate
**Faith:** Catholic, formally observant, privately indifferent
**Birthplace:** Sobań, family estate in Volhynia (within the Russian Empire proper, not the Congress Kingdom)

**Family situation.** Antoni is the second son of a junior branch of the Sobański family. His father served in the Russian diplomatic corps; his mother died when he was nine. He was raised in the houses of relatives in Saint Petersburg, in Vienna, and at Sobań. He has an older brother who manages the estate; Antoni inherited the Warsaw mansion on Senatorska Street and a comfortable income. He is unmarried, despite the efforts of half a dozen mothers across two empires.

**Position.** He holds an honorary chamberlainship at Constantine's court at Belweder — a position acquired through his father's connections, requiring his presence at certain functions and his polite cooperation with the imperial administration. He is also unofficially useful to senator Novosiltsev's circle: he drinks with Polish officers, attends salons, and reports — in selective and politically careful terms — what he hears. He considers himself a realist, not a traitor. The Polish cause, in his view, is romantic and doomed; cooperation will ensure that something Polish survives within the Russian frame.

**The dilemma.** Antoni meets Marianne in late September with the cynical intent of a brief seduction. By mid-October he is unsettled. By early November he is — to his bewilderment — in love. Her father is an old enemy of his father's circle. Her brother is precisely the kind of officer Antoni's contacts would arrest. To pursue her honorably means to break with the only social and political world he understands. The cracks in his carefully maintained dandyism widen as the political situation darkens.

**Voice and personality.** Antoni speaks beautifully — French, Polish, Russian, German, all fluent. He is funny, sardonic, allusive. His Polish is touched by St. Petersburg cadences (a slight formality, occasional Russified phrasing) that mark him to a sharp ear as a man who did not grow up in Warsaw. He uses irony as armor; when the irony cracks, his speech becomes plainer, almost halting. He is widely read in French Enlightenment authors and pretends not to be moved by Polish Romantic poetry, though he is.

**Key beats:**
- The first scene at the Krasiński Palace salon.
- A duel he would prefer to avoid (with Stefan Dembowski, Marianne's brother).
- A briefing with Novosiltsev where he is explicitly asked to report on the Dembowski household.
- The November 29 night sequence, with three radically different paths depending on the player's prior choices.

**Localization notes.** Antoni's source-language Polish includes occasional Russian phrases (*душа моя*, *хорошо*) used affectionately or carelessly. These should be transliterated from Cyrillic in target text where the source uses Cyrillic, and retained as Russian (not translated). His French is similarly preserved. Code-switching is character-defining. See Style Guide §6 for handling of multi-language utterances.

## Supporting Cast

### Józef Dembowski
Marianne's father. 56 years old. Former captain in the Polish 4th Vistula Legion, veteran of the 1812 campaign, returned with a saber wound in the left thigh. Manages the estate badly because he refuses to accept that the world has changed since 1815. Loving but distant father. Drinks his evening vodka in the library and tells stories of Smoleńsk. **Voice:** older Polish, slightly archaizing, peppered with military Latin and the occasional French command. Calls everyone *mości panie* or *mościa pani*.

### Zofia Dembowska
Marianne's mother. 49. Born Komorowska. Manages the estate's finances in a notebook that no one is allowed to read. She is the actual reason the family has not collapsed. **Voice:** spare, practical, sometimes sharp.

### Stefan Dembowski
Marianne's older brother. 23. Lieutenant in the Polish 4th Line Infantry Regiment, garrisoned at the Sapieha Barracks on Zakroczymska Street. Came home from the Cadet School two years ago for an officer's commission. Has old friendships with several of the conspirators, particularly through his classmate Józef Zaliwski. **Voice:** military Polish, direct, with traces of cadet slang. Adores his sister and is awkward about it.

### Helenka Dembowska
Marianne's younger sister. 12. Bright, observant, treats the political crisis as an exciting story unfolding around her. Has a quiet crush on Tadeusz when she meets him.

### Wojciech Lipiński
Tadeusz's father. 58. Apothecary, third generation in the trade, prosperous in a modest way. Admires the szlachta less than his son does and is troubled by Tadeusz's social ambitions. **Voice:** Warsaw townsman speech, blunt, salted with apothecary vocabulary.

### Maria Lipińska
Tadeusz's mother. 53. Quiet, devout, runs the household. Worried about her son.

### Dr. Henryk Reszka
Chief physician at the Bonifratrów Hospital, Tadeusz's mentor. 64. Trained in Padua in his youth, served in the Napoleonic medical corps, has been a member of patriotic networks for thirty years. He recruits Tadeusz with a slow patience. **Voice:** educated, Latin-heavy, ironic. Moves between medical authority and grandfatherly warmth.

### Sister Cecylia
Lay nurse at the hospital, Bernardine tertiary. Mid-fifties. Has been at Bonifratrów longer than any of the doctors. Tadeusz initially patronizes her; he learns better. **Voice:** simple, direct, devout.

### Anna Sobańska
Antoni's distant cousin and confidante. 28. Married to an army officer who is conveniently always elsewhere. She runs one of the more interesting Warsaw salons. Knows everyone. Pretends to be a cynic; is not. **Voice:** quick, witty, French-inflected.

### Captain Pavel Volkov
Russian Imperial Army officer attached to Constantine's staff. 35. Sobański's main interlocutor at Belweder. Cultivated, multilingual, ostensibly friendly. **Voice:** elegant Russian-accented Polish; correct French; speaks Russian only when alone with other Russians.

### Father Marek Wiśniewski
Parish priest of the Dembowski village. 47. A real Mazovian peasant's son who became a priest. Patriotic without being inflammatory; cautious, kind, and observant of the family's troubles.

## Real Historical Figures Who Appear as Named NPCs

These appear briefly in scripted scenes. Writers must coordinate any new dialogue for these characters with the historical consultant.

### Grand Duke Constantine Pavlovich
Brother of Tsar Nicholas I. 51 in 1830. Commander-in-chief of the Polish Army, viceroy in all but name. Volatile, capricious, capable of warmth, married to a Polish noblewoman. The player meets him only as Sobański (one Belweder scene), or briefly glimpses him at a parade. **Voice:** German-accented French in social settings, parade-ground Russian when angry, halting Polish when forced. He should never be a cartoon. His Polish wife's influence on him is real and worth representing.

### Joanna Grudzińska, Princess of Łowicz
Constantine's wife. 35. Polish, Catholic, soft-spoken. Lives at Belweder. Has limited but real influence over Constantine's mood. Possible single scene with Marianne (currently TBD) at a charity event. **Voice:** quiet Polish, perfect French.

### Lieutenant Piotr Wysocki
The leader of the Cadet School conspiracy. 33. Idealistic, intense, somewhat humorless, a good officer and an indifferent politician. Stefan Dembowski may introduce Marianne to him at one point. **Voice:** intense Polish, military precision, romantic phrasing when discussing the cause.

### Senator Nikolai Novosiltsev
Russian senator, head of imperial security in Warsaw. 69. Hated by Poles, brilliant, drunken, cynical. Sobański's most powerful contact. The player encounters him through Sobański, in one scripted dinner scene. **Voice:** bored Russian-accented Polish; precise French; rare bursts of obscene Russian when drunk.

### General Józef Chłopicki
Veteran general, retired and politically inactive in autumn 1830 but soon to be drafted as the Uprising's reluctant first dictator. Mid-60s. Possible glimpse in Sobański's storyline. **Voice:** terse, military, deeply Polish.
